Where should one start with Marx?

The easiest place to start would be the manuscripts derived from speeches he gave to workers and communists, Wage Labour and Capital and Value, Price and Profit. As opposed to Marxist-Leninists, Marx himself did not see the proletariat as inferior, so these texts provide an accessible explanation of foundational Marxist ideas without simplification. Engels also wrote a short text explaining dialectics and historical materialism, Socialism: Utopian and Scientific. After these texts you can move on to reading Capital, Volume 1.
